I am a postdoctoral space physicist at the Sodankylä Geophysical Observatory, University of Oulu, Finland. My research investigates Traveling Ionospheric Disturbances (TIDs) — large-scale wave structures in the Earth's ionosphere — using a rapid-run ionosonde, EISCAT incoherent scatter radar, and GPS Total Electron Content measurements. My PhD thesis, completed in 2026, provides the most comprehensive statistical characterisation of TIDs at high latitudes to date.

Before joining the University of Oulu, I conducted foundational ionospheric research in East Africa, studying plasma irregularities, equatorial electrojet-driven E×B drifts, and radio wave scintillation at low-latitude stations in Ethiopia. This dual-region expertise — spanning polar high-latitude physics and equatorial ionospheric dynamics — gives my research a uniquely global perspective on space weather phenomena.

I have also contributed to the intersection of space physics and machine learning, applying deep convolutional neural networks to the automated scaling of ionogram parameters — opening new possibilities for real-time space weather monitoring at remote and polar observatories.

Research

Bridging high-latitude and equatorial ionospheric physics with modern data science and remote sensing.

High-Latitude TID Dynamics

Statistical characterisation of traveling ionospheric disturbances at the Sodankylä Observatory using rapid-run ionosonde data. Covering wave parameters, seasonal patterns, solar cycle dependence, and coupling with atmospheric gravity waves observed by EISCAT radar.

Ionosonde EISCAT Sodankylä

Machine Learning for Space Weather

Development and validation of deep convolutional neural networks for automatic ionogram parameter scaling (foF2, hmF2, MUF). Enables high-throughput, observer-independent processing of large ionogram archives at polar observatories.

Deep CNN Ionograms Space Weather

Equatorial Ionospheric Physics

Multi-instrument investigations of plasma irregularities, equatorial electrojet-driven E×B drifts, and radio wave scintillation over the East African sector. Using C/NOFS satellite, COSMIC radio occultation, and ground-based GNSS networks.

GPS TEC Plasma Irregularities East Africa

Research Interests
