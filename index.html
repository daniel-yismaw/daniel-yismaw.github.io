About Me

I am a space physicist at the Sodankylä Geophysical Observatory, University of Oulu, Finland, where my doctoral research investigates Traveling Ionospheric Disturbances (TIDs) — large-scale wave structures in the Earth's ionosphere — using a rapid-run ionosonde, EISCAT incoherent scatter radar, and GPS Total Electron Content measurements.

Before joining the University of Oulu, I conducted foundational ionospheric research in East Africa, studying plasma irregularities, equatorial electrojet-driven E×B drifts, and radio wave scintillation at low-latitude stations in Ethiopia. This dual-region expertise — spanning polar high-latitude physics and equatorial ionospheric dynamics — gives my research a uniquely global perspective.

More recently, I have contributed to the growing intersection of space physics and machine learning, applying deep convolutional neural networks to the automated scaling of ionogram parameters — opening new possibilities for real-time space weather monitoring at remote observatories.

Research Interests

My research bridges high-latitude and equatorial ionospheric physics, combining classical instrumentation with modern data science.

Traveling Ionospheric Disturbances

Characterising TID statistics, wave parameters, and atmospheric gravity wave coupling using rapid-run ionosondes and EISCAT incoherent scatter radar at high latitudes.

Equatorial Ionospheric Physics

Investigating plasma irregularities, E×B drift dynamics, and radio wave scintillation over the East African equatorial sector using multi-instrument observations.

GPS TEC & Space Weather

Deriving Total Electron Content from GPS/GNSS networks, COSMIC occultations, and C/NOFS satellite data to monitor ionospheric variability and solar activity effects.

Machine Learning for Space Data

Applying deep convolutional neural networks for automatic ionogram parameter scaling, enabling scalable and reliable space weather monitoring at polar and sub-polar observatories.
